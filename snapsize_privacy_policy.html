Photo Access & Processing

SnapSize's core function is compressing, resizing, cropping, and converting your photos. Here is precisely how photo access works:

All photo processing happens 100% on your device. Your original photos and compressed outputs are never uploaded to any server â€” ours or anyone else's.

Permissions requested:

  • READ_MEDIA_IMAGES (Android 13+) / READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE (Android 12 and below): Required to let you select photos from your gallery. We read photo data only when you actively select a file. We do not background-scan or monitor your gallery.
  • INTERNET: Required for Google AdMob to serve advertisements and for Google Play Billing to process purchases. Not used to upload your photos.
  • ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE: Used to detect whether your device is online or offline, which determines batch processing limits for free users.

What happens to your photos:

  • You select a photo (or up to 10 photos in batch) using Android's system photo picker.
  • The photo is read into device memory, processed (compressed / resized / cropped / converted), and the output is saved to your device's Pictures/SnapSize folder.
  • A metadata record (file sizes, timestamp, format) is saved to the local Room database on your device.
  • The original photo is not modified or deleted unless you explicitly use the "Delete Original" function.
  • Compressed photos can be shared to other apps using Android's standard share sheet. SnapSize uses a FileProvider to make the file temporarily accessible for sharing â€” it is not uploaded anywhere.
